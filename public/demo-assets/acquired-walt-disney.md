# The Walt Disney Company — Walt's Era (1923–1966)

## From a garage studio to an empire

- **1923** — Walt and Roy Disney found the Disney Brothers Cartoon
  Studio in their uncle's garage in Los Angeles.
- **1928** — *Steamboat Willie* premieres at the Colony Theatre:
  the first cartoon with fully synchronized sound, and the debut
  of Mickey Mouse.
- **1937** — *Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs*, the world's first
  feature-length cel-animated film, grosses **$418M** lifetime —
  the highest of any film released in Walt's era.
- **1940** — *Pinocchio* and *Fantasia* push the artistic ceiling
  of animation; the studio moves to Burbank.
- **1950** — *Cinderella* ($85M) revives the studio after the
  lean war years.
- **1955** — **Disneyland** opens in Anaheim on July 17 in front
  of 28,000 guests and a live ABC broadcast.
- **1964** — *Mary Poppins* becomes the studio's biggest
  live-action hit to date.
- **1965** — Walt quietly assembles ~27,000 acres of Florida
  swampland southwest of Orlando under shell companies.
- **1966** — Walt films the EPCOT pitch: an Experimental Prototype
  Community of Tomorrow, weeks before his death on December 15.

## Revenue snapshot

Annual revenue grew from **$6,000** (1923) to roughly **$70M**
(1966), with step-changes at Snow White (1937), Cinderella (1950)
and Disneyland's opening (1955).
